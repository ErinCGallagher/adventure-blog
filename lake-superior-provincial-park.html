A Quick Hike Before the Park!

I know i'm starting off a a blog post about Lake Superior Provincial Park with another provincial park but they are very close to one another so bear with me :)

After arriving late Thursday night in Sault Ste. Marie, my girlfriend's and I were eager to start hiking bright and early Friday morning! After all, we had been trapped in tiny Toronto condos all summer so what could be more exciting and different from our regular day to day! After about a 50 minute drive from our hotel, the Quattro hotel in Sault Ste. Marie, we arrived at Pancake Bay Provincial Park. Located across highway 17 from the campground is the start of The Lookout Trail. This trail is known for it's panoramic view of Lake Superior and Pancake Bay and I can confirm that we were not disappointed. The hike itself is was pretty easy but I suggest wearing closed toe shoes because there are a lot of exposed rocks and roots. Once you get to the end of the trail there are about 4 flights of steep stairs which take you to the 2 lookout platforms. We did the trail first in the morning and only came across 1 or 2 other groups of hikers so we had the lookouts all to ourselves! I would highly recommend this trail as a start for your trip because it gives you a great sense of just how big Lake Superior really is.

Hike Details

Name: The Edmund Fitzgerald Lookout Trail

Distance: 7 km return, 1.5-2 hours

Terrain: Relatively Flat dirt trail with exposed rock and roots

Level: Easy

We may have only just started our trip but we knew how important a good meal was going to be after a great hike! Although the Agawa Fish & Chips truck doesn't look like anything special on the outside, they made our favourite fish and chips on the trip. Yes, this wasn't our last fish and chips stop. The fish is locally caught Lake Trout and whitefish and battered to perfection. Not too thick but not too thin! Their tartar sauce is also amazing, and this is coming from someone who generally just goes with ketchup.
